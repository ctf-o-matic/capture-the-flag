Capture The Flag

Because sometimes the best defense is a good offense

The hardest part of writing secure code is learning to think like an attacker. For example, every programmer is told to watch out for SQL injections, but it's hard to appreciate just how exploitable they are until you've written a SQL injection of your own.

We built Capture the Flag, a security wargame inspired by SmashTheStack's IO, to help our teams practice identifying and exploiting common security problems. After completing this CTF, you should have a greatly improved understanding of how attackers will try to break your code (and hopefully you will have fun in the process!).

You can begin the CTF challenge by running ssh -p 8022 level1@104.197.36.163 from your shell.

Your goal is to read the contents of /home/level1/.password. In /levels/level1, you'll find a binary owned by level1 (as well as its source code) â€” you will probably find it useful. Once you have the password, you can ssh in as level1, where new instructions await you.
